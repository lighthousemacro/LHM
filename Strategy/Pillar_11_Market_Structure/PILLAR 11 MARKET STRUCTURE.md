# PILLAR 11: MARKET STRUCTURE

## The Market Structure Transmission Chain

Market structure isn't just price action. It's the mathematical verdict on whether risk is being rewarded. Structure is where all 10 fundamental pillars get priced. It's the market's real-time ruling on labor, liquidity, credit, and everything else.

```
Trend Integrity → Momentum Confirmation → Breadth Participation →
Relative Strength → Price Discovery →
Trend Integrity (Reinforcing Loop)
```

**The Insight:** Trend, momentum, and breadth either confirm or diverge. When generals advance without soldiers (price up, breadth down), the rally is living on borrowed time. Structure breakdown precedes fundamental confirmation by 2-4 months.

The beauty of structure data: it can't be spun. Price is price. Breadth is breadth. Either 70% of stocks are above their 50-day MA or they're not. You can massage GDP with inventory builds. You can engineer earnings with buybacks. But you can't fake breadth. The market votes with its capital, and the ballots are already counted.

---

## Why Market Structure Matters

Market structure is the **final arbiter** in the 12-pillar framework for a simple reason: it's where capital flows meet price discovery. The fundamentals tell you *what should happen*. Structure tells you *what is happening*.

**The Cascade:**

**1. Structure → Confirmation:** Strong structure confirms fundamental thesis (follow the signal)
**2. Structure → Warning:** Weak structure despite strong fundamentals = distribution (be cautious)
**3. Structure → Leading:** Structure often breaks before fundamentals deteriorate (2-4 month lead)
**4. Structure → Positioning:** Structure determines whether to be aggressive or defensive
**5. Structure → Timing:** Entry/exit timing depends on trend and momentum state

Get the structure call right, and you've triangulated when to act. Miss it, and you're trading ideas instead of reality.

**The Divergence Edge:** When structure diverges from fundamentals, one of them is wrong. Structure is usually right earlier. When breadth collapses while price holds (SBD > +1.0), the generals are advancing without soldiers and distribution is underway. When breadth improves while price languishes (SBD < -1.0), accumulation is underway.

## Primary Indicators: The Complete Architecture

### A. TREND COMPONENTS (The Foundation)

Trend is the primary filter. Everything else is subordinate to trend. If price is below the 200-day MA, you're fighting the tape.

**The Timeframe Stack:** 20d → 50d → 200d. Each tells you something different:
- **20d (1 month):** Immediate momentum, entry timing, early warning
- **50d (1 quarter):** Intermediate trend, primary trading timeframe
- **200d (1 year):** Primary trend, bull/bear regime

|---|---|---|---|
| **Price vs 200d MA (%)** | Derived | Daily | Primary trend filter (+/- = above/below) |
| **Price vs 50d MA (%)** | Derived | Daily | Intermediate trend filter |
| **Price vs 20d MA (%)** | Derived | Daily | Short-term trend / entry timing |
| **20d MA Slope** | Derived | Daily | Immediate momentum direction |
| **50d MA Slope** | Derived | Daily | Trend acceleration (positive = rising) |
| **200d MA Slope** | Derived | Daily | Primary trend direction |

#### Derived Trend Metrics

| **Metric** | **Formula** | **Threshold** | **Signal** |

**The 200d Kill Line:** Below the 200d MA with recent break (<20 days) and expanding momentum, no trend-follow longs. Period. The data doesn't care about your fundamental thesis.

## Market Structure Index (MSI)

The MSI synthesizes trend, momentum, breadth, and relative strength into a single composite across multiple timeframes.

### Formula

```
MSI = 0.15 × z(Price_vs_200d_MA_%)
    + 0.10 × z(Price_vs_50d_MA_%)
    + 0.10 × z(50d_MA_Slope)
    + 0.10 × z(20d_MA_Slope)
    + 0.12 × z(Z_RoC_63d)
    + 0.10 × z(%_Stocks_>_50d_MA)
    + 0.08 × z(%_Stocks_>_20d_MA)
    + 0.08 × z(%_Stocks_>_200d_MA)
    + 0.07 × z(Net_New_Highs_Lows_20d_Avg)
    + 0.05 × z(AD_Line_50d_Slope)
    + 0.05 × z(McClellan_Summation)
```

### Weight Rationale

| **Component** | **Weight** | **Rationale** |
|---|---|---|
| Price vs 200d | 0.15 | Primary trend filter |
| Price vs 50d | 0.10 | Intermediate trend position |
| 50d Slope | 0.10 | Trend acceleration |
| 20d Slope | 0.10 | Immediate momentum direction |
| Z-RoC | 0.12 | Momentum confirmation |
| % > 50d MA | 0.10 | Primary breadth gauge |
| % > 20d MA | 0.08 | Short-term participation (thrust detection) |
| % > 200d MA | 0.08 | Trend health |
| Net NH-NL (20d) | 0.07 | Leadership expansion |
| A/D Slope | 0.05 | Cumulative participation |
| McClellan Sum | 0.05 | Breadth thrust detection |

### MSI Interpretation

| **MSI Range** | **Regime** | **Structural Allocation** | **Signal** |
|---|---|---|---|
| > +1.0 | Strong Uptrend | Full trend-follow exposure | Structure confirming |
| +0.5 to +1.0 | Bullish | Normal risk-on | Healthy participation |
| -0.5 to +0.5 | Transitional | Reduced exposure | Mixed signals |
| -1.0 to -0.5 | Weak | Defensive tilt | Structure deteriorating |
| < -1.0 | Bearish | No trend-follow longs | Structure broken |

## Structure-Breadth Divergence (SBD)

SBD measures the gap between price strength and participation. It's the "generals vs soldiers" indicator.

### Formula

```
SBD = z(Price_vs_200d) - z(%_Stocks_>_50d_MA)
```

### Interpretation

| **SBD Range** | **State** | **Interpretation** | **Action** |
|---|---|---|---|
| > +1.5 | Extreme Distribution | Price elevated, breadth collapsed | High alert, reduce |
| +1.0 to +1.5 | Distribution | Generals advancing, soldiers lagging | Caution, trail stops |
| -0.5 to +1.0 | Aligned | Price and breadth in sync | Normal positioning |
| -1.5 to -0.5 | Accumulation | Price weak, breadth improving | Watch for turn |
| < -1.5 | Extreme Accumulation | Capitulation, breadth diverging up | Contrarian buy zone |

**The SBD Signal:** When SBD > +1.0, the market is hollow: a small number of stocks are carrying the index. When SBD < -1.0, the market is healthy under the surface despite weak headlines.

## Sentiment-Structure Divergence (SSD)

SSD is the cross-pillar bridge between Pillar 11 (Market Structure) and Pillar 12 (Sentiment & Positioning). It captures when sentiment extremes align with structure extremes to signal capitulation lows or blow-off tops.

### Formula

```
SSD = z(SPI) + z(MSI)
```

Where SPI is the Sentiment & Positioning Index (Pillar 12) and MSI is the Market Structure Index (this pillar).

### Interpretation

| **SSD Range** | **State** | **Interpretation** | **Action** |
|---|---|---|---|
| > +1.5 | Capitulation Low | Extreme fear + broken structure aligned | Contrarian long setup (highest conviction) |
| +0.5 to +1.5 | Washout | Fear building + structure weak | Watch for turn |
| -0.5 to +0.5 | Aligned | Sentiment and structure in sync | Normal positioning |
| -1.5 to -0.5 | Complacency | Euphoria building + structure extending | Warning, tighten stops |
| < -1.5 | Blow-Off Top | Extreme euphoria + extended structure | Contrarian short setup (reduce aggressively) |

**Why SSD matters:** Sentiment extremes without structure confirmation are premature. Structure extremes without sentiment confirmation are incomplete. Both together is the highest-conviction setup because it combines positioning (what people are doing) with price action (what the market is actually saying).

**Historical calibration:**
- **March 2009:** SSD > +2.5 (AAII Bear-Bull at +51, MSI < -1.8). Generational low.
- **March 2020:** SSD > +2.0 (VIX 82, Put/Call 1.5, MSI -1.5). COVID bottom.
- **October 2022:** SSD > +1.5 (AAII Bear 56%, MSI -1.2). Cycle low.
- **January 2000:** SSD < -1.8 (AAII Bull 75%, MSI +1.5, Price-50d +15%). Tech peak.
- **December 2021:** SSD < -1.5 (AAII Bull 55%, crowded breadth, meme activity). Cycle peak.

Both extremes are rare (2-3 occurrences per cycle), which is precisely why they're actionable.

## Trading Applications

### When MSI Confirms Fundamentals (Engines 1-2)

- **Both positive:** Maximum conviction, full position sizing
- **Fundamentals positive, MSI weakening:** Reduce exposure, trail stops
- **Fundamentals positive, MSI negative:** No new longs, wait for structure

### When MSI Diverges from Fundamentals

- **Fundamentals weak, MSI strong:** Structure likely wrong; follow fundamentals
- **Fundamentals strong, MSI weak:** Distribution underway; respect structure
- **Fundamentals neutral, MSI extreme:** Follow structure as tiebreaker

### Using SBD for Timing

- **SBD > +1.0 with MSI positive:** Late-stage rally, tighten stops
- **SBD < -1.0 with MSI negative:** Washout in progress, prepare for entry
- **SBD normalizing from extremes:** Trend continuation likely

## E. CROSS-ASSET STRUCTURE (Beyond US Equity)

Market structure isn't just an equity phenomenon. Rates, dollar, commodities, and credit all exhibit trend, momentum, breadth, and relative strength dynamics. Cross-asset structure provides context and early warning that pure equity structure misses.

### Cross-Asset Structural Signals

| **Signal** | **Interpretation** | **Cycle Position** |
|---|---|---|
| **Equities + Credit + Commodities all uptrend** | Broad risk-on regime | Mid-cycle expansion |
| **Equities uptrend, Credit spreads widening** | Early risk-off in credit | Late-cycle warning |
| **Dollar uptrend + Commodities downtrend** | Global liquidity tightening | Restrictive regime |
| **Rates downtrend + Equities uptrend** | Financial conditions loosening | Recovery or Fed cut cycle |
| **Rates downtrend + Equities downtrend** | Recession pricing | Flight-to-quality active |
| **Gold uptrend + Real rates up** | Currency/fiscal stress | Store-of-value demand |
| **Bitcoin uptrend + Equity downtrend** | Alternative asset rotation | Liquidity specific signals |

### Cross-Asset Correlation Regimes

Correlation matrices shift with regime. Understanding current correlation structure helps identify whether diversification is working.

**Low Correlation Regime** (normal): Equities/bonds negatively correlated. Diversification works.

**High Correlation Regime** (stress): All risk assets positively correlated. Diversification fails. This is the regime where risk parity strategies underperform and traditional 60/40 portfolios lose protection.

**Inflation Regime**: Rates, equities, and credit all correlate positively on inflation surprises. The 2022 correlation regime was inflation-driven; 60/40 experienced worst drawdown in decades.

## G. FACTOR STRUCTURE (Style Regimes)

Factor investing captures persistent return premiums associated with stock characteristics. Factor performance varies across cycles and provides additional structural signals.

### The Five Major Factors

| **Factor** | **Definition** | **ETF Proxy** | **Cycle Role** |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Value** | High BV/P, low P/E | IVE, RPV | Outperforms in reflation, recovery |
| **Growth** | High earnings growth | IVW, SPYG | Outperforms in low-rate mid-cycle |
| **Quality** | High ROE, stable earnings | QUAL, SPHQ | Outperforms late-cycle and recession |
| **Momentum** | Recent price trend | MTUM, PDP | Outperforms in trending markets |
| **Low Volatility** | Low historical vol | SPLV, USMV | Outperforms late-cycle, recession |
| **Size (Small-Cap)** | Smaller market cap | IWM, IJR | Outperforms early cycle |

### Factor Regime Detection

Factor regimes can be detected by relative strength shifts:

- **Risk-on, Growth leads:** Growth > Value, Small > Large, Momentum strong
- **Risk-on, Value leads:** Value > Growth, Cyclicals lead, reflation trade
- **Risk-off, Quality leads:** Quality > Growth, Low Vol > High Beta
- **Risk-off, Defensive:** Quality and Low Vol outperform, Momentum breaks

### Derived Factor Metrics

| **Metric** | **Formula** | **Threshold** | **Signal** |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Value-Growth Spread (YoY)** | IVE YoY - IVW YoY | >+5 ppts | Value regime |
| **Quality-Growth Spread** | QUAL YoY - SPYG YoY | >+5 ppts | Late-cycle defensive |
| **Momentum Drawdown** | MTUM drawdown from peak | >-10% | Momentum break |
| **Small-Large Spread** | IWM YoY - SPY YoY | <-10 ppts | Risk-off within equities |

## H. VOLATILITY STRUCTURE (Beyond VIX Level)

Volatility dynamics are a structural signal in their own right. The VIX is the starting point, but the volatility complex and its regime provide richer information.

### Volatility Regimes

| **Regime** | **VIX Level** | **VIX Term Structure** | **Dispersion** | **Interpretation** |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Compressed (Complacent)** | <13 | Steep contango | Low dispersion | Late-cycle mispricing |
| **Normal** | 14-20 | Normal contango | Normal dispersion | Mid-cycle calm |
| **Elevated** | 20-30 | Flat or backwardation | Rising dispersion | Stress emerging |
| **Stress** | 30-50 | Backwardation | High dispersion | Risk-off active |
| **Crisis** | >50 | Extreme backwardation | Stock-specific chaos | Systemic stress |

### Volatility Term Structure Signal

The VIX9D/VIX/VIX3M/VIX6M structure tells you about the *shape* of volatility expectations.

- **Steep Contango** (VIX9D < VIX < VIX3M < VIX6M): Calm conditions; vol sellers make money
- **Flat:** Transition regime
- **Backwardation** (VIX9D > VIX > VIX3M): Near-term stress concentrated; mean reversion trade setup
- **Curve Collapse** (VIX9D spikes to match VIX3M): Event-driven spike, typically reverses

### SKEW and Tail Risk

The CBOE SKEW Index measures pricing of tail risk (out-of-the-money put demand). SKEW above 145 is extreme tail hedging; below 115 is complacency.

### Volatility-Structure Integration

| **MSI State** | **Vol State** | **Interpretation** |
|---|---|---|
| Positive MSI + Low VIX | Healthy trending market | Full risk-on |
| Positive MSI + Rising VIX | Distribution underway | Warning, trail stops |
| Negative MSI + High VIX | Panic; capitulation setup | Contrarian opportunity |
| Negative MSI + Low VIX | Complacent decline | Worst configuration; further downside |

## I. OPTIONS POSITIONING STRUCTURE (Dealer and Flow Data)

Options flow data provides a real-time window into positioning. Dealer gamma positioning, put/call ratios, and dark pool activity are all structural data points.

### Put/Call Ratio Signals

The CBOE put/call ratio measures put volume vs call volume. High readings signal fear; low readings signal complacency.

| **Put/Call Ratio** | **Interpretation** |
|---|---|
| **>1.2** | Extreme fear (contrarian long) |
| **1.0-1.2** | Elevated fear |
| **0.8-1.0** | Normal |
| **0.6-0.8** | Complacency |
| **<0.6** | Extreme complacency (contrarian short) |

### Dealer Gamma Positioning

Dealer gamma is the aggregate gamma exposure of market makers. It influences how options flow gets hedged and thus affects underlying volatility.

- **Long Gamma Regime**: Dealers sell rallies and buy dips; suppresses vol
- **Short Gamma Regime**: Dealers buy rallies and sell dips; amplifies vol
- **Gamma Flip Level**: The S&P level where gamma shifts sign; acts as magnetic support/resistance

Watch firms like SpotGamma, Nomura (QDS), and SqueezeMetrics for gamma positioning estimates.

### Dark Pool Data

Dark pool print data (trades executed off-exchange) reveals institutional positioning that isn't visible in lit markets. Rising dark pool share signals institutional repositioning.

### Options Structural Metrics

| **Metric** | **Source** | **Threshold** | **Signal** |
|---|---|---|---|
| **CBOE Put/Call (10d avg)** | CBOE | >1.1 = extreme fear | Contrarian long |
| **Equity-Only Put/Call** | CBOE | >1.0 = fear | Oversold setup |
| **VIX Put/Call** | CBOE | >1.5 = VIX demand | Vol pumping |
| **Dark Pool % of Volume** | Finra ATS | >40% = institutional repositioning | Watch for follow-through |
| **Dealer Gamma Level** | SpotGamma / Nomura QDS | Negative = short gamma | Vol-amplifying |

| **Pattern** | **Large-Cap** | **Small-Cap** | **Interpretation** |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Unified Bullish** | Positive | Positive | Broad risk-on regime |
| **Large-Only Bull** | Positive | Negative | Narrow leadership warning |
| **Unified Bearish** | Negative | Negative | Broad risk-off |
| **Small-Only Bull** | Negative | Positive | Unusual; risk appetite returning |

**Small-cap leading is a late-correction signal.** When Russell 2000 MSI turns positive while SPX MSI remains weak, risk appetite is returning to the more speculative cohort. This historically precedes broad market turns by 1-3 months.

**Large-cap-only rallies are narrow.** When SPX MSI is positive while Russell 2000 MSI is negative, mega-cap concentration is driving the index. This is the 2023-2024 Mag 7 dynamic. The configuration isn't immediately bearish, but it's fragile because the narrow leadership requires those specific stocks to keep working.

## Integration with Three-Engine Framework

### Pillar 11 → Pillar 12 (Sentiment & Positioning)

Structure and sentiment form the market engine together. Structure captures price action; sentiment captures positioning. Both are needed to confirm regime changes.

**SSD (Sentiment-Structure Divergence):** The formal bridge metric. When sentiment extremes align with structure extremes, the highest-conviction setups form.

**Cross-Pillar Signal:** SSD > +1.5 (capitulation low) or SSD < -1.5 (blow-off top) signals highest-conviction contrarian trades. These occur 2-3 times per cycle.

### Pillar 11 → Pillar 9 (Financial)

Market structure and financial conditions interact. Healthy structure supports loose financial conditions via wealth effects; breaking structure tightens financial conditions via wealth destruction.

**Transmission mechanics:** A 10-15% equity drawdown reduces household wealth by $6-10T and contributes to spending contraction with 2-4 quarter lag (Pillar 5 wealth effect). Structure breakdown thus transmits to the real economy through financial conditions.

**Cross-Pillar Signal:** When MSI < -1.0 AND HY OAS > 400 bps, the wealth-destruction plus credit-stress combination accelerates recession transmission.

### Pillar 11 → Pillar 1 (Labor)

Structure signals corporate revenue expectations, which drive hiring plans. Persistent structural breakdown precedes layoff waves by 2-6 months.

**Transmission mechanics:** Equity-based compensation makes up a material share of total comp for professional services (tech, finance). Large equity declines compress the compensation pool, leading to layoffs and compensation restructuring within 2-3 quarters.

**Cross-Pillar Signal:** When MSI < -1.0 AND LFI > +0.8, layoff acceleration is likely within 2-4 quarters. Structure is the early signal; labor confirms.

### Pillar 11 → Pillar 3 (Growth)

Market structure leads GDP growth expectations. Historical pattern: MSI inflections lead GDP turning points by 3-6 months.

**Transmission mechanics:** Structure signals coordinated views on future cash flows. When breadth collapses and relative strength rotates defensive, the market is pricing slower growth ahead. The real economy then catches up through consumer wealth effects, business investment pullback, and hiring freezes.

**Cross-Pillar Signal:** MSI < -0.5 for 3+ months combined with GCI < 0 is a strong recession signal; both composites negative has preceded every post-1990 recession.

### Pillar 11 → Pillar 10 (Plumbing)

Structure and plumbing interact in both directions. Unbuffered plumbing (Pillar 10) amplifies structure breakdowns; structural stress can force Fed plumbing intervention.

**Transmission mechanics:** When market structure breaks, forced deleveraging (margin calls, risk parity de-risking, CTA signal reversal) drains liquidity. This combined with thin plumbing (LCI < -0.5) creates dislocation risk. The March 2020 Treasury dysfunction was an extreme example.

**Cross-Pillar Signal:** MSI < -1.0 AND LCI < -0.5 is the stress configuration most likely to produce forced Fed intervention.

## Invalidation Criteria

### Bull Case (Structure Remains Intact) Invalidation

If the following occur simultaneously for 2+ weeks, the bullish structure thesis is invalidated:

- Price closes below 200d MA on SPX
- 50d MA crosses below 200d MA (death cross)
- Z-RoC drops below -1.0 and stays there
- % stocks above 50d MA collapses below 35%
- MSI drops below -0.5
- VIX sustainably above 22 with backwardation

**Action if Invalidated:** Reduce equity exposure to defensive targets per MRI regime. No trend-follow longs. Tighten or remove existing long stops.

### Bear Case (Structure Remains Broken) Invalidation

If the following occur simultaneously, the bearish structure thesis is invalidated:

- Price sustainably above 200d MA (>20 days)
- 50d MA crosses above 200d MA (golden cross)
- Z-RoC above +1.0 sustained
- % stocks above 50d MA surges above 70% (breadth thrust)
- MSI above +0.5
- SSD moves from +1.5 range toward 0 (capitulation worked off)

**Action if Invalidated:** Resume trend-follow positioning. Add to cyclical exposure in confirmed relative strength leaders. Cover defensive/short positioning.

## Additional Indicators & External Research

### The 200-Day Moving Average Rule

The 200-day moving average is the single most-followed trend indicator. Its simplicity is its power: every institutional mandate, every CTA system, every trend-follower uses it (implicitly or explicitly) as a primary filter. The 200d break isn't just technical; it triggers mechanical position reduction across systematic strategies.

**Faber's Quantitative Timing Model (Cambria):** Meb Faber demonstrated that a simple 200d MA rule on diversified asset classes captures most of the return with substantially less drawdown. The rule is: long when price > 10M MA, cash when price < 10M MA. Applied to SPX, this rule missed the worst of 2000-2002, 2008-2009, and 2020 drawdowns.

### Zweig Breadth Thrust (ZBT)

The Zweig Breadth Thrust (ZBT) is one of the most reliable bull market start signals. Defined as NYSE advances / (advances + declines) 10-day MA going from below 0.40 to above 0.615 within 10 trading days.

**Historical track record:** ZBTs have occurred approximately 12 times since 1950 (including 2023). Every occurrence has been followed by substantial gains over the subsequent 12 months (typical range +20-40%).

### Lowry's 90% Up Days and Down Days

Lowry Research identifies "90% days" as sessions where 90%+ of volume or 90%+ of points traded are on one side (up or down). Clusters of 90% down days signal climactic selling; clusters of 90% up days signal climactic buying.

**Pattern:** Multiple 90% down days within a short window (5-10 trading days) often precedes major bottoms. A 90% up day within 3-5 days of the down cluster often confirms the turn.

### McClellan Oscillator Signals

The McClellan Oscillator is a derivative of NYSE advances/declines: 19-day EMA minus 39-day EMA of daily net advances. Readings above +100 are overbought; below -100 are oversold.

**Cumulative version (McClellan Summation):** Rising Summation Index from below -1000 toward positive territory has historically been a durable bull signal.

### The Coppock Curve

The Coppock Curve is a monthly long-term momentum indicator: sum of 14-month and 11-month rate-of-change, smoothed by 10-month weighted moving average. Positive crosses from negative territory have signaled major bull market starts (1942, 1948, 1982, 2003, 2009, 2020).

### The Hindenburg Omen

The Hindenburg Omen is a specific technical configuration signaling market top risk: (1) 52-week NH and NL both above 2.2% of NYSE issues simultaneously, (2) McClellan Oscillator negative, (3) 10-week MA rising. Multiple triggers within a short window have preceded significant corrections.

**Caveat:** The Hindenburg Omen has a high false-positive rate when triggered in isolation. Consider as a contributing warning signal rather than definitive.

### Dow Theory

Charles Dow's original theory: primary trend requires confirmation from both Dow Industrials and Dow Transports. When one makes new highs without the other, divergence warns of trend exhaustion. Modern analog: SPX + small-cap + transports (IYT) all hitting new highs = broad bull confirmation.

### Stock-Bond Correlation Regime

The 60-day rolling correlation between SPX and 10Y Treasury yield has regime dynamics:

- **Deflation regime** (most of 2000-2020): Negative correlation. Risk-off sends money to bonds; risk-on sells bonds.
- **Inflation regime** (2022-present): Positive correlation. Rising rates hurt both stocks and bonds simultaneously.

Regime identification affects portfolio construction. In inflation regimes, 60/40 diversification doesn't work; consider alternatives (commodities, gold, trend-following).

### The Absolute Trading Rules Background

Bob's framework relies on five Absolute Trading Rules derived from Pillar 11 structural signals. These rules are hard filters that override fundamental thesis.

1. **Below 200d MA rule**: No trend-follow longs below 200d
2. **Death cross rule**: 50d below 200d = no new longs (with overrides possible)
3. **Red relative rule**: No longs in assets with red relative strength panel
4. **Z-RoC broken rule**: Exit positions when Z-RoC < -1.0
5. **Extension rule**: No entries when price > +15% above 50d MA

These rules are structural enforcement of the broader framework's risk management.

### Volume Analysis

Volume confirms price action. Rising price on rising volume is healthy; rising price on falling volume signals distribution. However, volume analysis is less reliable in the post-2010 era due to market structure changes (dark pools, passive flows, algorithmic trading).

Use volume primarily for confirmation of breakout/breakdown levels rather than as standalone signal.

### False Signals

**August 2015 China Devaluation:** MSI dropped sharply, SBD went to +1.4 (distribution). Yet Fed pivoted dovish by September, and markets recovered within weeks. Structure signal was real but short-duration; Fed response time determined the outcome.

**September 2018:** MSI dropped to -0.5 with rising breadth divergence (SBD +0.5). Looked like distribution, but holiday Q4 drawdown was deep enough that the correction fully washed structure (MSI to -1.2 by December), which was the actionable signal rather than September's warning.

**May-June 2022:** MSI already broken (-0.8), but bear market rally in June-August pushed MSI back toward 0 briefly. Brief MSI recoveries in bear markets are common head-fakes; the 200d downtrend test and subsequent failure confirms the structural regime.

### Structural Breaks

**Post-2010 Passive/ETF Era:** Rise of index funds and ETFs has changed breadth dynamics. Breadth measures now partially reflect index flow rather than individual stock conviction. Interpret breadth signals with awareness of passive flow influence.

**Post-2020 Retail Flow:** Gamma squeezes, meme stocks, and zero-DTE options trading have changed intraday market structure. Weekly options expirations now dominate near-term positioning. Classical breadth indicators still work over multi-month horizons but are noisier intraday.

**Mag 7 Concentration:** Post-2022 mega-cap concentration has made index-level breadth measures misleading. SBD remains valid but with increased sensitivity; RSP (equal-weight) vs SPX (cap-weighted) divergences have become structural norm rather than late-cycle warning.

**CTA and Trend-Follower Positioning:** Systematic trend-followers (CTAs) have grown to meaningful AUM. Their positioning creates feedback effects: when price breaks 200d, CTAs short; when it crosses back up, they cover. This creates magnetic levels around key MAs that weren't as pronounced pre-2010.
